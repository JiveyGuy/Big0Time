There is a common misconception about logarithmic graphs that needs to be addressed so that the reader does not misunderstand data presented within this document.

⑆ X-Axis Vs. 1-Line

        Many people when first seeing a logarithmic graph confuse the 1-line for the X-axis of a normal (X, Y) coordinate graph. It is important to realize that logarithmic graphs are used to display data, that is sometimes order of magnitude apart, in a meaningful way. Sometimes numbers in the millions place[1] will be compared to numbers in the thousandths places, so it will be necessary to split the data into two subsections. Where one section is less than 1, and the other is greater than 1.
